Efficient Walking Robot

Abstract

Traditional robotic legs are often inefficient due to the mismatch between motor capabilities and the varying torque requirements of walking. This project demonstrates an Efficient Walking Robot leg prototype that utilizes reconfigurable hydrostatics to overcome these limitations.

By dynamically reconfiguring the hydraulic connections and integrating passive force mechanisms, the system can track ground reaction force profiles for walking, running, and jumping with extreme efficiency. Experimental results show that this approach achieves up to 4.8x lower energy consumption during walking compared to traditional fully actuated designs, paving the way for long-range autonomous legged robots and wearable devices.
