Low-Adhesion Vehicle Control

Abstract

Autonomous vehicles face significant challenges in low-adhesion conditions such as snow and ice. We are developing smart vehicle controllers that adapt their behaviors dynamically according to road conditions.

Our research focuses on a hybrid control architecture that leverages the best of both worlds: a robust model-based baseline for safety, combined with learning-based event reflexes for complex maneuvers. By utilizing physics-informed features and dimensionless numbers, we aim to create universal controllers that can generalize maneuvers across different vehicle platforms and environments. A custom 1/5th scale test car equipped with independent motors on each wheel is used for experimental validation.
